IMIQ Project - Detailed Summary and Key Sections Translation
Executive Summary
Project Title: IMIQ - Intelligent Mobility Space in the District (Intelligenter Mobilitätsraum im Quartier)
Two Main Components:

IMIQ-RIM: Real Laboratory for Intelligent Mobility
IMIQ-DWLZ: Digital Work-Life Twin

Location: Science Harbor (Wissenschaftshafen), Magdeburg
Lead Institution: Otto-von-Guericke University Magdeburg

Project Goals (Section 1b)
Main Objective
Develop and test innovative mobility and communication approaches that create a holistic mobility and communication experience through sensors, 5G, and digital services, while promoting social interaction locally.
Key Challenges Addressed:

Increasing traffic volume in Germany with high share of motorized individual traffic
Demographic change and urbanization
Economic and ecological challenges - balancing low costs with flexibility and low emissions
Safety improvements - reducing accidents and fatalities
Digitalization of mobility services (Mobility-as-a-Service)

Future Mobility Characteristics:
The project aims to develop mobility that is:

Efficient and organized
Electric
Automated
Connected
Safe
Collectively and universally usable


Our Approach - Two Main Research Strands
A: Real Laboratory for Intelligent Mobility (RIM)
Core Features:

Autonomous shuttle bus operation in Science Harbor district
Integration with University campus via public roads
Level 4 SAE autonomy (fully autonomous in defined conditions)
Remote Operation Control Center (OCC) monitoring
Comprehensive sensor infrastructure
5G test field for real-time communication

Key Technologies:

Autonomous Vehicles:

Small electric shuttle buses
Large-scale line buses (12m)
Cargo transport robot vehicles
Complete handover processes for goods


Infrastructure:

Road Side Units (RSU) throughout the district
5G/G5 communication networks
V2X (Vehicle-to-Everything) technology
Comprehensive sensor systems (cameras, LiDAR, radar)


Operation Control Center:

Remote monitoring of all autonomous vehicles
Manufacturer-independent platform
Real-time communication with passengers
Teleoperation capabilities for precise positioning


Data Infrastructure:

Real-time data capture and processing
Integration with Digital Work-Life Twin
AI and machine learning for continuous improvement
"Learning real laboratory" for intelligent mobility



B: Digital Work-Life Twin (DWLZ)
Purpose:
Creates a virtual model combining movement and business processes, socio-technical participation processes, and existing/future aspects of the Science Harbor district.
Goes Beyond Traditional Digital Twins:

Not just machine/logistics bidirectional real-time connection
Includes socio-technical and anticipatory co-development
Integrates user needs and behaviors
Enables scenario simulation and system optimization

Development Stages (Maturity Model):
Stage 1 - Mapping:

Map mobility-related objects
Use real usage and observation data
Build co-twin for all private and business mobility processes
Continuous RIM data and infrastructure integration
Collect needs scenarios for different user groups
Use Big Data and Data Analytics
Develop personalized IT solutions

Stage 2 - Simulation:

Simulate processes and scenarios
Test business models and services
Integrate new participation formats for user groups
Implement needs-based participation possibilities
Use AI, algorithms, and app development
Conduct "what-if" scenarios for mobility
Consider air quality with sensors

Stage 3 - Personalization:

Integrate individual users personally
Use BDI agents (intelligent virtual actors)
Optimize movement profiles in Science Harbor
Develop user-specific personalized interfaces (DIN EN ISO 9241 Usability)
Create interfaces to the "4Ws" (Living, Business, Science, Wellbeing)
Integrate practical scenarios meeting user needs

Key Technologies and Methods
RIM Technologies
1. Autonomous Vehicles:

SAE Level 4 (high automation in defined conditions)
Various vehicle types: shuttle buses, line buses (12m), cargo robots
Equipped with sensors (LiDAR, cameras, radar)
Electric drive systems

2. Sensor Systems:

Mono cameras
360° Multi-Layer LiDAR systems
Long-Range 3D/4D Imaging Radars
Complementary sensor systems
Weather and light-resistant through sensor fusion
Local preprocessing for privacy protection
Track-level fusion

3. Communication:

5G campus network (first in Saxony-Anhalt)
ITS-G5 (WLAN-based vehicle networking)
V2X (Vehicle-to-Everything) technology
RSU (Road Side Units) throughout district
Real-time data transmission

4. Operation Control Center:

Remote monitoring and control
Manufacturer-independent platform
ROS/ROS2 programming language
Real-time passenger communication
Teleoperation for precise positioning
VDV guideline compliance

5. Infrastructure:

High-resolution 3D mapping of entire district
Physical route preparation for autonomous driving
Digital infrastructure modernization
Mobility stations
Environmental sensors (weather, particulate matter, air quality)

DWLZ Technologies
1. Software Architecture:

Integration of various domain data

3. Human Modeling:

5. Simulation:
